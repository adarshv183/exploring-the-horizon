NASA APOD

NASA APOD stands for NASA's Astronomy Picture of the Day. It is a popular website and daily publication that features a different astronomical image or photograph each day. NASA APOD is managed by a team of professional astronomers who select and provide explanations for the featured images. These images typically showcase a wide range of celestial objects and phenomena, including stars, galaxies, planets, nebulae, and more.

LIVE ISS Tracker

The ISS, or International Space Station, is a large, habitable spacecraft that orbits the Earth in low Earth orbit (LEO). It serves as a space laboratory, observatory, and living quarters for astronauts and cosmonauts from various nations The tracker shows where the Space Station is right now and its path 90 minutes ago (-1.5 hr) and 90 minutes ahead (+1.5 hr). The dark overlay indicates where it is nighttime in the world.

Mars Rover Images

"Mars rover images" refer to photographs and visual data captured by robotic rovers that NASA and other space agencies have sent to explore the planet Mars. These rovers are equipped with cameras and scientific instruments designed to capture and transmit images of the Martian surface, atmosphere, and geological features. These images provide valuable insights into the planet's geology, climate, and potential habitability.
